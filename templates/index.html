Beyond Surveys: A Machine Learning Approach to Understanding Precision Agriculture Adoption in the U.S.

Abstract

This study introduces a semi-automated, data-driven framework to analyze and compare the adoption of precision agriculture technologies across U.S. states. By leveraging historical data and unsupervised machine learning, we identify trends in technological adoption and their relationship to crop productivity and sales.

“Our approach validates USDA surveys using empirical growth data — a step toward transparent, evidence-based agricultural analytics.”

Project Goals

Use clustering to detect economic and tech performance patterns.
Normalize historical datasets for fair cross-state comparison.
Highlight how precision tools relate to yield improvement.
Provide data-driven insights to help shape policy and training.

Michigan in Cluster Analysis

Our clustering algorithm placed Michigan in Cluster 4 — a group defined by:

  • Consistent but moderate economic growth
  • Early adoption of tech but not full saturation
  • Mid-tier yield improvements

This cluster reveals opportunities for targeted investment and support in infrastructure and farmer education.
